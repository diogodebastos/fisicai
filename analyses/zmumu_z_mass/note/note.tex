\begin{abstract}
The dimuon invariant-mass spectrum is reconstructed from a skim of the CMS
\texttt{DoubleMuParked} primary dataset recorded in 2012 at $\sqs = 8\TeV$ and
released as Open Data. Events with exactly two opposite-charge muons with
$\pt > \PtMin\GeV$ and $\abseta < \EtaMax$ are selected, and the position of the
Z resonance peak is extracted with a binned $\chi^{2}$ fit of a Voigtian signal
plus an exponential background in the $\FitLo$--$\FitHi\GeV$ range. The fitted
peak position is
$\mmumu^{\text{peak}} = \MZPeak \pm \MZPeakStat\stat \pm \MZPeakSystRange\syst\GeV$,
where the systematic uncertainty covers only the fit-range variation. The result
lies $\PeakMinusPdgPermille$ per mille below the world-average Z-boson mass, as
expected in the absence of muon momentum-scale and final-state-radiation
corrections; no competitive Z mass measurement is claimed.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The decay \Zmm provides one of the cleanest signatures at a hadron collider: a
narrow resonance at $m_{\mathrm{Z}} = \MZPdg \pm \MZPdgErr\GeV$
\cite{ParticleDataGroup:2024cfk} on top of a small, smoothly falling Drell--Yan
continuum and misreconstruction background. This note documents a self-contained
determination of the dimuon peak position in proton--proton collision data
recorded by the CMS detector \cite{CMS:2008xjf} and released through the CERN
Open Data portal \cite{Lassila-Perini:2021xzn}.

The emphasis is on reproducibility: every number quoted in this note is
generated by \texttt{analysis.py} and imported as a \LaTeX{} macro from the
accompanying \texttt{results.json}, so the note and the analysis code cannot
drift apart. The full regeneration procedure is given in the bundle
\texttt{README}.

\section{Detector and data}

CMS is a general-purpose detector at the CERN LHC, featuring a silicon tracker
and muon chambers embedded in a $3.8\,$T solenoidal field; muons are
reconstructed by combining tracker and muon-system information, with coverage up
to $\abseta = \EtaMax$ \cite{CMS:2008xjf}.

The input is \texttt{zmumu\_skim.root}, a skim of the CMS 2012
\texttt{DoubleMuParked} primary dataset ($\sqs = 8\TeV$) published on the CERN
Open Data portal and converted to a flat NanoAOD-like format with the CMS
\texttt{AOD2NanoAODOutreachTool} \cite{Lassila-Perini:2021xzn}. The skim
contains \NEventsTotal{} events with per-muon branches \texttt{Muon\_pt},
\texttt{Muon\_eta}, \texttt{Muon\_phi}, \texttt{Muon\_mass}, and
\texttt{Muon\_charge}. Only raw event counts are used: no luminosity
normalization, pile-up reweighting, or trigger-efficiency correction is applied,
none of which affects the position of the resonance peak.

\section{Event selection}

Muons are required to have $\pt > \PtMin\GeV$, well onto the trigger plateau of
the double-muon parked stream, and $\abseta < \EtaMax$, matching the
muon-system acceptance \cite{CMS:2008xjf}. Events must contain exactly two such
muons, and the pair must have opposite charge, as expected for \Zmm decays. The
dimuon invariant mass \mmumu is computed from the four-momentum sum of the two
muons, and the spectrum is analyzed in the
$\MassWindowLo \le \mmumu < \MassWindowHi\GeV$ window. The cutflow is given in
Table~\ref{tab:cutflow}.

\section{Analysis method}

The selected masses are histogrammed in $\BinWidthGev\GeV$ bins and fitted by
minimizing a $\chi^{2}$ with Poisson bin uncertainties, using the model
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\mathrm{d}N}{\mathrm{d}m} \;=\; n_{\text{sig}}\,
  V\!\left(m - \mu;\, \sigma,\, \Gamma_{\mathrm{Z}}/2\right)
  \;+\; n_{\text{bkg}}\, f_{\text{bkg}}(m),
\end{equation}
where $V$ is a Voigtian --- a Breit--Wigner convolved with a Gaussian resolution
of width $\sigma$ --- and $f_{\text{bkg}}$ is a falling exponential normalized
over the fit range, so that $n_{\text{sig}}$ and $n_{\text{bkg}}$ are yields in
that range. The Breit--Wigner width is fixed to the world-average Z width
$\Gamma_{\mathrm{Z}} = \GammaZPdg\GeV$ \cite{ParticleDataGroup:2024cfk}, while
the peak position $\mu$, the resolution $\sigma$, the two yields, and the
background slope are floated. The nominal fit uses the $\FitLo$--$\FitHi\GeV$
core of the peak, excluding the low-mass tail populated by final-state
radiation (FSR), which the symmetric Voigtian does not model.

\section{Systematic uncertainties}

The only systematic uncertainty that is evaluated is the sensitivity of the
peak position to the choice of fit range: the fit is repeated with three
alternative ranges ($80$--$100$, $83$--$99$, and $86$--$96\GeV$, as defined in
\texttt{analysis.py}), and the largest absolute shift of the fitted peak,
$\MZPeakSystRange\GeV$, is taken as the uncertainty.

The following are explicitly \emph{not} evaluated: the muon momentum-scale and
resolution calibration (the dominant systematic in a genuine Z mass
measurement), the modeling of FSR, the choice of signal parameterization (a
symmetric Voigtian with $\Gamma_{\mathrm{Z}}$ fixed) and of the background
shape, and alignment- or charge-dependent momentum biases. For this reason the
result is presented as a determination of the reconstructed peak position, not
as a Z mass measurement.

\section{Results}

The nominal fit converges with $\chi^{2}/\text{ndf} = \ChiTwo/\Ndf = \ChiTwoNdf$
and yields
\begin{equation}
  \mmumu^{\text{peak}} \;=\;
  \MZPeak \;\pm\; \MZPeakStat\stat \;\pm\; \MZPeakSystRange\syst\GeV,
\end{equation}
with a Gaussian resolution $\sigma = \SigmaRes \pm \SigmaResStat\GeV$ and fitted
yields of $\NSigFit \pm \NSigFitStat$ signal and \NBkgFit{} background events in
the fit range. The spectrum and fit are shown in Fig.~\ref{fig:dimuon_mass}.

Compared with the world-average Z-boson mass
$m_{\mathrm{Z}} = \MZPdg \pm \MZPdgErr\GeV$ \cite{ParticleDataGroup:2024cfk},
the difference $\mmumu^{\text{peak}} - m_{\mathrm{Z}}$ is $\PeakMinusPdgMev\MeV$,
i.e.\ $\PeakMinusPdgPermille$ per mille. The magnitude is comparable to the
combined quoted uncertainties, and the sign is the one expected from the two
dominant effects deliberately left uncorrected: unrecovered FSR photons, which
shift the reconstructed peak below the pole, and the uncalibrated muon momentum
scale of this open-data skim.

\section{Summary}

The \Zmm resonance is reconstructed in the CMS 2012 \texttt{DoubleMuParked}
Open Data skim, with \NEventsMassWindow{} selected events in the
$\MassWindowLo$--$\MassWindowHi\GeV$ window, and its peak position is determined
to be $\MZPeak \pm \MZPeakStat\stat \pm \MZPeakSystRange\syst\GeV$, in
per-mille-level agreement with the world-average Z mass. The result validates
the public skim, the selection, and the fitting chain; it does not claim a
competitive Z mass measurement, since the muon momentum scale and FSR are left
uncorrected. The entire analysis is reproducible from \texttt{analysis.py} as
described in the bundle \texttt{README}.
